--- title: Entrants, leavers and transfers knit: govukhugo::govukhugo_knit date: 2021-01-01 weight: 400 summary: Headline statistics on movements into, out of and within the Civil Service rmarkdown: true ---

Each year thousands of people join and leave the Civil Service, as well as move between organisations within the Civil Service. In the year to 31 March 2021 there were 57,150 entrants to the Civil Service, 27,580 leavers from the Civil Service, and 11,910 transfers within the Civil Service1.

{{< details "More information on types of movement">}} Entrants are those who have joined a Civil Service organisation and were not working elsewhere in the Civil Service.

Leavers are those who leave a Civil Service organisation and do not go on to work elswehere in the Civil Service.

Transfers are those who move from one Civil Service organisation to another, there are three main types of transfer:

  • a Machinery of Government (MoG) change, where an area of work moves from the responsibility of one organisation to another, this is recorded in the Civil Service Statistics as a "Transfer of Function Within the Civil Service";
  • a permanent transfer, where a civil servant moves to a new job in another organisation on a permanent basis, this is recorded the Civil Service Statistics as "Transfer to Another Department"; and,
  • a loan, where a civil servant moves to a new job in another organisation on a temporary basis, this is recorded in the Civil Service Statistics as either "Loan To Another Department" (when the individual leaves starts the loan and works for the host organisation) or "End of Loan" (when the individual ends the loan and returns to their home organisation). {{< /details >}}

Leavers

The largest reason for leaving the Civil Service is resignation (accounting for 10,780 leavers) followed by retirement (accounting for 7,580 leavers), together they are responsible for 66.6% of all leavers from the Civil Service in the year to 31 March 2021.

Transfers

In the year to 31 March 2021, the majority of transfers within the Civil Service were due to individuals moving to another role in another Civil Service Organisation (9,610, 80.7%).
