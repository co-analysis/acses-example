--- title: Civil servants by grade date: 2021-01-01 weight: 100 summary: Headline statistics of Civil Servants by grade rmarkdown: true ---

As at 31 March 2021 three in ten civil servants (30.0%) were in an administrative grade1, compared to almost half of civil servants (47.1%) in 2011 being at an administrative grade. There are now 7,290 civil servants are at Senior Civil Service Level2, this equates to 1.6% of the Civil Service.

Grade by organisation

Organisations with large numbers of front-line and operational staff delivering public services tend to have a higher proportion of junior grades. HM Prison and Probation Service has the largest proportion of staff at an administrative grade (67.7%), while Department for Work and Pensions (excl. agencies) has the largest number of staff at an administrative grade (29,320).

Organisations with more senior grades tend to have fewer employees delivering services directly to the public. Competition and Markets Authority has the largest proportion of staff at Senior Civil Service Level (12.9%), while Cabinet Office (excl. agencies) has the largest number of staff at Senior Civil Service Level (970)3.

  1. Proportions and percentages are of those with a known grade. There were 16,570 staff whose grade was not reported, this is equivalent to 3.4% of all employees.↩︎

  2. There are two measures for the most senior grades: the Senior Civil Service, and Senior Civil Service Level. See the statistical notes for more detail.↩︎

  3. This analysis excludes organisations with less than 250 employees overall.↩︎
