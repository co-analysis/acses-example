As at 31 March 2021, there were 156,270 civil servants who declared their religion or belief as Christian, this equates to 47.2% of those who declared their religion or belief. There were43,400civil servants who declared having another religion or belief (13.1% of those who declared their religion), and 43,400civil servants who declared they have no religion (13.1% of those who declared their religion).1

The proportion of staff at all grades who report they are a Christian is similar across all responsibility levels, staff at junior grades are slightly less likely to declare they have no religion and more likely to declare they have another religion than those at senior grades.

  1. The Civil Service Statistics record two types of missing value for religion or belief. Where an individual has specifically chosen not to provide their religion or belief, for example by selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not declared". Where an individual has not provided any data on their religion or belief, including not selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not reported". There are 154,050 individuals whose religion or belief is either not declared or not reported, which is 31.8% of total headcount.↩︎
