As at 31 March 2021, there were 18,830 who declared their sexual orientation as either lesbian, gay, bisexual or something else other than heterosexual/straight, this equates to 5.6% of those who declared their sexual orientation.1

  1. The Civil Service Statistics record two types of missing value for sexual orientation. Where an individual has specifically chosen not to provide their sexual orientation, for example by selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not declared". Where an individual has not provided any data on their sexual orientation, including not selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not reported". There are 149,080 individuals whose sexual orientation is either not declared or not reported, which is 30.7% of total headcount.↩︎
