As of 31 March 2021, there were 50,560 civil servants that reported they were disabled (equating to 13.6% of those whose disability status is known).1

At the Executive Officer responsibility level 15.2% have declared they are disabled, almost double the proportion of those at Senior Civil Service Level that declared they are disabled (7.9%).

The three departmental groups with the largest proportion of staff reporting they have a disability are: Work and Pensions (18.1%); United Kingdom Statistics Authority (16.9%) and, Scottish Government (14.9%). The three departmental groups with the smallest proportion of staff reporting they have a disability are: Welsh Government (6.1%); Digital, Culture, Media and Sport (8.3%) and, Health and Social Care (8.3%).

Where an individual has not provided any data on their disability status, including not selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not reported".↩︎
