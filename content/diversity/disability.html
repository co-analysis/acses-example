As of 31 March 2021, there were 50,560 civil servants that reported they were disabled (equating to 13.6% of those whose disability status is known).1

At the Executive Officer responsibility level 15.2% have declared they are disabled, almost double the proportion of those at Senior Civil Service Level that declared they are disabled (7.9%).

  1. The Civil Service Statistics record two types of missing value for disability status. Where an individual has specifically chosen not to provide their disability status, for example by selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not declared". Where an individual has not provided any data on their disability status, including not selecting a "Prefer not to say" value, this is recorded as "Not reported".↩︎
