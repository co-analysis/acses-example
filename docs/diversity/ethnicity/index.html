Civil servants by ethnicity and national identity

As at 31 March 2021 there were 340,400 civil servants that reported have a white ethnicity (equating to 85.7% of those with a known ethnicity) and there were 56,860 civil servants that reported the were from an ethnic minority background (equating to 14.3% of those with a known ethnicity).

Around one in nine of those at Senior Civil Service Level are from an ethnic minority background, compared to around one in six of those at the Executive Officer responsibility level.

The three departmental groups with the largest proportion of staff from an ethnic minority background are: International Trade (26.2%); Health and Social Care (25.9%) and, Home Office (23.7%). The three departmental groups with the smallest proportion of staff from an ethnic minority background are: Scottish Government (2.9%); Welsh Government (3.0%) and, Defence (6.2%).
