Civil servants by ethnicity and national identity

This page provides statistics on civil servants by ethnicity and national identity

Ethnicity

As at 31 March 2021 there were 340,400 civil servants that reported have a white ethnicity (equating to 85.7% of those with a known ethnicity) and there were 56,860 civil servants that reported the were from an ethnic minority background (equating to 14.3% of those with a known ethnicity).1

  • Table

Around one in nine of those at Senior Civil Service Level are from an ethnic minority background, compared to around one in six of those at the Executive Officer responsibility level.

The three departmental groups with the largest proportion of staff from an ethnic minority background are: International Trade (26.2%); Health and Social Care (25.9%) and, Home Office (23.7%). The three departmental groups with the smallest proportion of staff from an ethnic minority background are: Scottish Government (2.9%); Welsh Government (3.0%) and, Defence (6.2%).

National identity

As at 31 March 2021 there were 11,510 civil servants that had a national identity from outside the United Kingdom2, equating to 3.8% of those with a known national identity.3

  • Table

The proportion of staff with known national identity who declare a national identity from outside the United Kingdom is broadly similar across responsibility levels. However, at junior responsibility levels data on national identity is less likely to be available than for those at senior levels.
